Retail Sales Analysis Dashboard (Excel Project)


🎯 Objective

The objective of this project is to analyse retail transaction data in order to identify key patterns in customer behaviour, product performance, and sales trends across different segments such as age group, gender, and product category.

🛠 Tools Used

Microsoft Excel

Pivot Tables

Charts & Dashboard Design

Data Cleaning & Transformation


📊 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains retail transaction data including:

Transaction ID

Date

Customer ID

Gender

Age

Product Category

Quantity

Price per Unit

A calculated field (Total Sales = Quantity × Price per Unit) was created for analysis.


🔄 Process Followed

Cleaned raw dataset by removing duplicates and fixing data formats

Created new feature: Total Sales

Created Age Groups for segmentation

Built pivot tables to analyse:


Revenue by Product Category

Revenue by Age Group

Revenue by Gender

Monthly Sales Trends

Designed an Excel dashboard to visualise key insights


📊 Key Insights

Electronics is the highest revenue category (£156,905), followed closely by Clothing (£155,580) and Beauty (£143,515)

The 36–50 (£139,660) and 50+ (£133,310) age groups generate the highest total revenue

The 26–35 age group is the strongest younger segment with £98,480 in total spend

Female customers contribute slightly higher revenue (£232,840) compared to male customers (£223,160)

Sales show seasonal variation with noticeable peaks in May and October

The 18–25 age group contributes the lowest total revenue (£84,550)


💡 Recommendations

Focus marketing strategies on 26–35 and 36–50 age groups due to higher spending behaviour

Increase targeted campaigns for the 18–25 segment to improve engagement

Optimise stock and promotions during peak months (May and October)

Strengthen Electronics category strategy due to consistent high performance across segments

Explore cross-category bundling to increase average transaction value
